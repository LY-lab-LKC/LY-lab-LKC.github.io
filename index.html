About Us

Ischemic heart disease (IHD), resulting from cardiac muscle damage following myocardial ischemia, is a debilitating and costly chronic condition with limited curative treatments available. Since 2013, Lynn has spearheaded the development of safe cellular products for regenerative cardiology. Using our patented laminin-221 protocol, we derived cardiovascular progenitors (CVPs) from human pluripotent stem cells (hPSCs). Our work demonstrated that intramuscular transplantation of CVPs into myocardial infarction (MI) mouse and pig hearts successfully formed muscle bundles, enhanced heart function, and reduced ventricular arrhythmia incidence by 50% (Cell Reports, npj, and bioRxiv)

Our research seeks to uncover the molecular and physiological mechanisms behind stem cell-based therapies for regenerative cardiology. We employ cell and molecular techniques, genome editing, metabolic assays, animal models, transcriptomics, proteomics, and bioinformatics to create a comprehensive RNA and protein atlas for target discovery.

Our program focuses on:

  1. Mechanism of laminins in cardiac differentiation
  2. Investigating inflammatory responses, cell engraftment and viability, graft maturation, fibrosis resolution, and ventricular tachycardia mitigation in vivo
  3. Understanding the efficacy of CVPs in metabolic animal models
  4. Developing methods to encapsulate CVPs in laminin-hydrogel and exploring catheter transplantation



These advancements aim to transform clinical practices and improve treatment for end-stage heart patients.
